# 🍽️ Zomato Restaurant Data Analysis

## 📌 Project Overview

This project performs **Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)** on a Zomato restaurant dataset using Python. The objective is to uncover customer preferences, restaurant trends, pricing patterns, and business insights that can support data-driven decision-making.

The analysis includes data cleaning, visualization, and interpretation of restaurant ratings, customer votes, spending patterns, and online ordering trends.

---

## 🎯 Objectives

The objectives of this project are to:

* Clean and preprocess the dataset.
* Explore restaurant categories and customer behavior.
* Analyze restaurant ratings and customer votes.
* Study customer spending patterns.
* Compare online and offline ordering trends.
* Generate business insights and recommendations through data visualization.

---

## 📂 Dataset

The dataset contains information about restaurants listed on Zomato, including:

* Restaurant Name
* Restaurant Type
* Customer Rating
* Number of Votes
* Approximate Cost for Two People
* Online Ordering Availability

## 📈 Business Questions Answered

This project explores the following questions:

1. Which restaurant type is most common?
2. Which restaurant type receives the highest number of customer votes?
3. What ratings do most restaurants receive?
4. What is the average spending for two people?
5. Do restaurants offering online ordering receive better ratings?
6. Which restaurant types receive more offline orders?
7. Which restaurant categories have the highest average ratings?
8. Is there a relationship between restaurant cost and customer ratings?
9. Which restaurants are the most popular based on customer votes?

---

## 💡 Key Insights

* Dining restaurants are the most common category on Zomato.
* Dining restaurants receive the highest customer engagement through votes.
* Most restaurants have ratings between **3.5 and 4.0**.
* The average spending for two people is approximately **₹300**.
* Restaurants offering online ordering generally receive higher customer ratings.
* Restaurant cost has only a weak relationship with customer ratings.
* Customer satisfaction appears to depend on multiple factors beyond price.
